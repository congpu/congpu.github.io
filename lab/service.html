    Outreach Activity

    Week of Welcome, Applied Engineering Complex, Marshall University, August 20, 2021.

    Purpose: Week of Welcome (WOW) is part of the First Year Experience at Marshall University. WOW serves as more than a class or program; it is an experience that is essential to the success of first-year students at Marshall. It is a collaborative, introductory effort to welcome, educate and socialize freshmen to the Marshall University campus, academic expectations, personnel, resources and traditions during the first few weeks of the fall semester.

    GenCyber: Adventure Zone Teacher Academy 2021, Marshall University, June 14-18, 2021.

    Purpose: GenCyber: Adventure Zone Teacher Academy includes a one-week virtual session summer camp that will assist teachers in developing and integrating cyber subjects into their courses at their home schools. Camp participants will engage in hands-on explorations of the many aspects of security including ethics, networks, phishing, social engineering, and robotics system. Camp participants will also develop lesson plans for delivering cybersecurity content in K-12 curricula. We are intending to train twenty high school teachers at Marshall University.

    Chair of Computer Science Symposium on Emerging Technologies (CSSET) 2021, Marshall University, April 26, 2021.

    Purpose: The Computer Science Symposium on Emerging Technologies (CSSET) 2021 is a university-wide event and aims at bringing the professors, researchers, and students together in the fields of computer science, information systems, and cyber security to disseminate original research results, and share new ideas and emerging technologies through practical development experience concentrating on both theory and practices. CSSET is also held to allow industry and government recruiters to reach out to computer science and computer & information security students at Marshall University for their hiring needs during busy seasons and to secure future talent with internships.

    Summer 2020 Virtual Orientation, Marshall University, July 21-24, 2020.

    Purpose: The orientation sessions serve as the formal introduction to the #MarshallUFamily and provide information about all things Marshall. Students can expect an engaging online experience that will include group sessions, live Q&A, academic college meetings with deans and academic advisors, student life and housing information, additional resource information and the opportunity for one-on-one contact with members of the Marshall community—all focused on preparing new students to become Sons and Daughters of Marshall.

    Green and White Day, Recreation Center, Marshall University, February 17, 2020.

    Purpose: Green and White Day is an event intended for high school juniors and seniors who would like a more in-depth look at what Marshall University has to offer and includes a full day of programming. Students and parents will have the opportunity to meet with professors, tour campus and hear from current students about all things Marshall.

    The Class of 2024 Reception for Admitted Students, Arthur Weisberg Family Applied Engineering Complex, Marshall University, January 18, 2020.

    Purpose: The Class of 2024 Reception gives admitted students the opportunity to meet and greet members of the Marshall University Community.

    2019 Coding & Cyber Summit, Charleston, WV, December 3, 2019.

    Purpose: Key industry and education leaders in the Mountain State have joined forces to focus on development of a cyber workforce talent pipeline. Efforts to date will be highlighted, and tech experts will share best practices and industry standards. Advertise Computer Science programs at Marshall University, and showcase student research projects from mentored research, class projects, and independent work.

    Air Force Association CyberPatriot Summer Camp, Huntington, WV, June, 2019.

    Purpose: Inspire K-12 students toward careers in cybersecurity or other science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) disciplines critical to nation’s future.

    Privacy and Cyber Security Day, Capital, Charleston, WV, February 01, 2019.

    Purpose: Interact with state agencies and various organizations to see how cyber security initiatives play into their day to day operations and allow them to emphasize the importance of what that means to them and the clientele they serve.

    2018 WV EXPO – West Virginia's Construction & Design Industries, Charleston, WV, March 22, 2018.

    Purpose: Advertise Computer Science programs at Marshall University, and showcase student research projects from mentored research, class projects, and independent work.

    Discover Engineering Day, Clay Center, Charleston, WV, February 24, 2018.

    Purpose: Local colleges, universities and organizations offering STEM career opportunities gather at the Clay Center and set up booths with hands-on activities that help introduce students and their adult caregivers to the world of STEM.

    VeX Robotics Meeting in St. Joseph High School, Huntington, WV, November, 2017

    Purpose: Provide help and training to high school for robotics competition.

    Engineering and Computer Science Camp, West Texas A&M University. Canyon, TX, June 2012

    Purpose: Provide an opportunity for middle school students to learn about what each of the engineering fields do, build and test equipment and conduct experiments in the various disciplines that include all of STEM.

    Culture Exchange Activity with Primary School, Amarillo, TX, April 2010

    Purpose: Provide an opportunity for primary school kids to develop a greater understanding of diversity outside of US.
