Cong Pu is an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Marshall University. He received Ph.D. (2016) and M.S. (2013) in Computer Science from Texas Tech University, TX, USA and B.S. (2009) in Computer Science and Technology from Zhengzhou University, China. Before his current appointment, he was an Instructor in the Dept. of Computer Science at Texas Tech University from 2014 and 2016 when he worked towards Ph.D. degree. He is a member of the IEEE and IEEE Communication Society. He was the recipient of the Helen DeVitt Jones Excellence in Graduate Teaching Award at Texas Tech University. He was the winner (faculty coach) of 2017 Design for Delight (D4D) Innovation Challenge Competition (Marshall University and Intuit Inc.). He received Open Education Resources (OER) Grant Award (West Virginia’s Higher Education Policy Commission and the Community and Technical College System). He was the member of Computer Science Workgroup for West Virginia Department of Education, and also was in the National Advisory Committee for the ETS Computer Science Praxis Exam.

His primary research interests include Cryptography, Network Security, Wireless Networks, Mobile Computing, and Information-Centric Networking, particularly Internet of Things, Internet of Drones, Flying Ad Hoc Networks, Named Data Networking, Energy Harvesting Motivated Networks, Mobile Ad Hoc Networks, Low Power and Lossy Networks, and Evacuation Assisting Vehicular Networks. He received NASA EPSCoR Research Seed Grant and NASA West Virginia Space Grant Consortium Research Initiation Grant. His research has also been extensively supported by various grant awards (i.e., John Marshall University Summer Scholars Award, Marshall University Summer Research Award, etc) from Marshall University. He served as a technical program committee member in many international conferences. He was a reviewer for many IEEE, ACM, and Elsevier journals. He is also serving as Associate Editor of several journals.

As a faculty member at Marshall University, he actively works on each of the followings:

  • Teaching: He teaches three courses, plus online course(s). He develops course materials, administers and teaches courses, grades student works, and responds to student requests.
  • Academic Advising: He advises students on curricular requirements and course schedules related to registration for courses.
  • Research Advising: He supervises undergraduate and graduate students on research project. He meets with students regularly to advise their projects and provide constructive feedback to students' ideas, writings, and presentations.
  • Research Publication: He works on new research subjects, develops new project ideas, analyzes, evaluates, and simulates and/or implements ideas and algorithms, and writes and revises research papers.
  • Fundraising: He writes grant proposals to get funding for his research lab. He also assists his students with applying various scholarship and fellowship to support their research work.
  • Academic Professional Service: He serves as reviewer, technical program committee, and associate editor to review papers and coordinate the careful and critical review of articles.
  • Department, College, and University Service: He participates in Department, College, University, and Community service activities. He organizes professional Department events, and participates in various College and University recruitment activities.

Recruitment: He is recruiting self-motivated and hard-working Graduate/Undergraduate students who are interested in Cryptography, Network Security, Wireless Network & Mobile Computing, Internet of Things, and/or Internet of Drones to work on research project.
Research Collaboration: Research scholars are always welcome for co-work.
